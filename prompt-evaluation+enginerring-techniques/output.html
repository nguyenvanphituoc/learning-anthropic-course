# One-Day Marathon Training Meal Plan for Carbohydrate Loading

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete aiming to build endurance and replenish glycogen stores for marathon training:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 4,200 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (140g), Fat (110g), Carbs (580g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Breakfast (7:00 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with honey (40g), banana (150g), and whole milk (300g). Toast with almond butter (25g).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 110g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (10:00 AM):** Sports drink (500ml) with electrolytes and carbohydrates, and an energy bar (65g).
    * Protein: 8g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 85g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Pasta (120g dry weight) with marinara sauce (150g), grilled chicken breast (150g), olive oil (15ml), and a side salad with mixed greens (120g) and olive oil dressing (15ml).
    * Protein: 42g, Fat: 18g, Carbs: 110g

* **Pre-Training Snack (3:30 PM):** White rice cakes (80g) with jam (40g) and banana (120g).
    * Protein: 6g, Fat: 2g, Carbs: 95g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** Baked sweet potato (300g), lean ground turkey (160g), steamed carrots (150g), and rice (80g dry weight cooked). Olive oil (10ml) for cooking.
    * Protein: 38g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 125g

* **Evening Snack (9:30 PM):** Greek yogurt (200g) with granola (50g) and honey (20g).
    * Protein: 22g, Fat: 58g, Carbs: 55g

This meal plan emphasizes high-quality carbohydrates (pasta, rice, sweet potatoes, oats) to maximize glycogen storage while providing adequate protein for muscle recovery and repair. The timing of meals supports both training performance and post-training recovery.
